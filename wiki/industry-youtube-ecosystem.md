# YouTube Ecosystem

## How YouTube Works as a Business
YouTube is both a platform and a revenue-sharing network. Creators and media companies earn money through:
- **Ad revenue** — YouTube pays ~55% of ad revenue to the channel owner via the YouTube Partner Program (YPP)
- **Channel memberships** — recurring fan subscriptions
- **Super Chat / Super Thanks** — direct fan payments during livestreams
- **Merchandise shelf** — integrated product sales
- **Brand deals** — sponsorships negotiated directly with brands

## Multi-Channel Networks (MCNs)
MCNs like Little Dot Studios aggregate multiple YouTube channels under one management umbrella. They provide:
- Content strategy and optimization
- Ad sales and monetization management
- Copyright management (Content ID)
- Production support
- Analytics and reporting

In exchange, MCNs take a revenue share from the channels they manage.

## Content ID
YouTube's copyright system that lets rights holders (studios, sports leagues, music labels) claim ad revenue on user-uploaded content that matches their catalog. Little Dot Studios manages Content ID on behalf of clients — a significant revenue stream for rights holders and a core LDS service.

## YouTube Analytics Key Metrics
- **Views** — raw viewcount
- **Watch time** — total minutes watched (key for algorithm ranking)
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — % of impressions that result in a click
- **AVD (Average View Duration)** — average time watched per view
- **RPM (Revenue Per Mille)** — revenue per 1,000 views (creator-side)
- **CPM (Cost Per Mille)** — what advertisers pay per 1,000 impressions
- **Subscriber growth / churn**

## CTV and FAST Channels
**CTV (Connected TV)** — streaming on smart TVs (Roku, Fire TV, Apple TV, etc.)
**FAST (Free Ad-Supported Streaming TV)** — free channels with ad breaks, like Pluto TV, Tubi, Samsung TV Plus. Little Dot operates 6 FAST channels.

FAST is growing rapidly as viewers cut the cord. Ad rates on FAST are generally higher than YouTube (closer to traditional TV CPMs).

## Relevance to BI Role
LDS's revenue is tied to channel performance metrics. The BI Analyst would likely need to understand:
- Which channels/content types drive the most ad revenue
- CPM trends by content category and platform
- Client channel health reporting
