# Digital Media Landscape

## Key Trends

### Cord-Cutting
Traditional TV viewership declining. Audiences moving to streaming (SVOD, AVOD, FAST). This drives demand for digital-native content companies like Little Dot Studios.

### FAST Channel Growth
Free Ad-Supported Streaming TV is one of the fastest-growing segments. Platforms: Pluto TV, Tubi, Peacock Free, Samsung TV Plus, Roku Channel. Ad rates approaching traditional TV CPMs ($15–40 CPM vs YouTube's $2–10).

### Short-Form vs Long-Form
- **Short-form** (YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels) — high volume, lower CPMs, algorithmic distribution
- **Long-form** (YouTube, CTV) — deeper engagement, higher CPMs, better brand safety

### Creator Economy
Independent creators increasingly work with agencies/MCNs for monetization, brand deals, and production support. LDS benefits from this as a full-service partner.

### Sports Rights in Digital
Sports federations are increasingly distributing content directly on YouTube and social media rather than relying solely on broadcast deals. LDS's sports agency work (WING) taps into this.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Jukin Media** — viral content licensing
- **Studio71** — MCN (now part of Red Arrow Studios)

## Revenue Models in Digital Media
| Model | Description | LDS Exposure |
|---|---|---|
| Ad revenue share | % of YouTube/CTV ad revenue | High (core business) |
| Agency retainer | Monthly fee for channel management | High |
| Production fee | Fixed/day rate for content creation | High |
| Licensing | Fees for distributing content | Medium (21K hours licensed) |
| Subscription | SVOD recurring revenue | Low (History Hit only) |
