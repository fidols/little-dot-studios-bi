# Resource & Capacity Planning

## What It Is
Resource planning = matching the right people with the right projects at the right time. Capacity planning = understanding how much work your team can take on without burning out or missing deadlines.

## Key Concepts

### Utilization Rate
Billable hours / total available hours. The primary health metric for a services business.
- **Target:** 65–75% for most agencies (leaving room for admin, training, new business)
- **>85%** = team is overloaded, quality and morale at risk
- **<50%** = team is underutilized, margin is suffering

### Capacity
Total available hours across the team in a given period. Example: 10 people × 40 hrs/week × 4 weeks = 1,600 hrs/month capacity.

### Demand Forecasting
Estimating how many hours each upcoming project will require, broken down by role/skill. Used to decide: can we take on this project with current staff, or do we need to hire/freelance?

### Staffing Models
- **Fixed staff** — predictable cost, lower margin flexibility
- **Freelancer-heavy** — variable cost, scales with demand, harder to manage quality
- **Hybrid** — core permanent team + freelance surge capacity (most common in production)

## Common Problems
1. **Double-booking** — same person assigned to two projects simultaneously
2. **Skill gaps** — project requires a skill no one on the team has
3. **Feast/famine cycles** — too much work one month, not enough the next
4. **Reactive hiring** — only hiring when already overloaded (too late)

## Tools
- **Float** — dedicated resource planning tool for agencies
- **Resource Guru** — capacity planning and scheduling
- **Smartsheet / Monday.com** — project management with resource views
- **Excel/Sheets** — still widely used for capacity modeling

## The BI Analyst's Role
JD: "Support the development and evaluation of future staffing and resourcing models, bringing a data-driven perspective to capacity planning and team design."

In practice: build models that show current utilization, forecast future demand based on pipeline, and recommend hiring vs. freelance decisions.
