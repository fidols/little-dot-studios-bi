# Profitability Analysis

## Project-Level P&L in Media/Production
Each project at an agency or production company has its own mini profit & loss statement:

```
Revenue (client fee or ad share)
- Direct costs (freelancer fees, production costs, licensing)
- Allocated labor (internal team hours × loaded cost rate)
- Overhead allocation
= Gross Profit
Gross Margin % = Gross Profit / Revenue
```

## Key Concepts

### Loaded Cost Rate
The true cost of an employee's time including salary, benefits, taxes, and overhead. Typically 1.25–1.5x base salary. Used to convert time tracking hours into dollar cost.

### Billable vs. Cost Rate
- **Billable rate** — what you charge the client per hour
- **Cost rate** — what it actually costs the company per hour
- The difference is gross margin per hour

### Project Margin Benchmarks (Agency/Production)
- **<20% gross margin** — unprofitable or at risk
- **20–40%** — typical range for production projects
- **40%+** — healthy agency retainer work

### Contribution Margin
Revenue minus variable costs only (no overhead allocation). Useful for evaluating whether a project/client is worth keeping even if it doesn't fully cover overhead.

## Budgeting in Production Companies
- **Top-down budgeting** — Finance sets revenue targets, teams work backward to staffing
- **Bottom-up budgeting** — teams estimate hours needed, Finance aggregates to revenue/cost projections
- **Rolling forecasts** — updated monthly based on actuals vs. plan

## Common Analysis the BI Analyst Runs
1. **Client profitability ranking** — which clients generate the most margin?
2. **Project type analysis** — are production projects more profitable than retainer work?
3. **Budget vs. actual variance** — which projects came in over/under budget and why?
4. **Margin trend over time** — is profitability improving or degrading as the business scales?
