# BI Analyst Role — Little Dot Studios

## Position
**Title:** Analyst, Business Intelligence
**Team:** Commercial Planning
**Location:** Los Angeles (remote-first)
**Salary:** $70,000–$80,000
**Experience:** 2–4 years

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Time Tracking Platform Ownership
- Own and manage the company's project time-tracking system
- Ensure accurate data capture and consistent usage
- Drive ongoing process improvement
- See: [Time Tracking Analytics](concept-time-tracking.md)

### 2. Time vs. Plan Analysis
- Analyze actual hours against project plans and delivery expectations
- Identify trends, inefficiencies, and resourcing gaps
- See: [Resource & Capacity Planning](concept-resource-planning.md)

### 3. Finance Partnership
- Provide operational data and assumptions for forecasting, budgeting, and profitability analysis
- See: [Profitability Analysis](concept-profitability.md)

### 4. Pricing & New Business
- Serve as pricing point of contact for new business and renewal pitches
- Collaborate on staffing assumptions and delivery models

### 5. Staffing & Resourcing Models
- Support development of future staffing models
- Bring data-driven perspective to capacity planning and team design

### 6. Project-Level Insights
- Highlight performance drivers, risks, and opportunities
- Deliver recommendations, not just reports

### 7. Workflow & Efficiency Analysis
- Analyze task-level workflows across teams
- Identify opportunities to reduce friction and improve productivity

### 8. CRM & Client Data
- Own client data files and CRM management
- Track client behavior, engagement trends, and commercial activity
- See: [CRM Analytics](concept-crm-analytics.md)

### 9. Dashboards & Reporting
- Build dashboards and reporting tools for cross-functional stakeholders
- Make complex data accessible and actionable

### 10. Ad Hoc Analysis & Presentations
- Support strategic initiatives with analysis and business-focused presentations

## Required Skills
- Advanced Excel
- BI tools (Tableau, Power BI, Looker — not specified)
- Time-tracking systems (Harvest, Toggl, Clockify — not specified)
- CRM platforms (Salesforce, HubSpot — not specified)
- Google Suite
- Statistical thinking, A/B testing awareness
- Clear communication to non-technical stakeholders

## Key Interview Angles
1. **"Tell me about a time you found an inefficiency in a process"** — maps to workflow/time tracking analysis
2. **"How have you supported financial planning with data?"** — maps to Finance partnership
3. **"How do you make data accessible to non-technical stakeholders?"** — maps to dashboards/presentations
4. **"How do you manage competing priorities?"** — maps to multi-team support (Finance + Ops + Commercial)
