# Source: Little Dot Studios 2026 YouTube Viewing Whitepaper

**Sources:** tubefilter.com, quasa.io, littledotstudios.com
**Published:** February 2026
**Ingested:** 2026-05-12

## Data Set
- 800+ managed YouTube channels
- 930 million subscribers
- 11.2 billion monthly views

## Key Findings

### Video Length vs. Revenue
| Video Length | % of Uploads | % of Watch Time | % of Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Under 30 min | 61% | 18% | 30% |
| 30+ min | 39% | 82% | 70% |
| 45–60 min (sweet spot) | — | — | ~40% of total revenue |

**Implication:** Short-form content is high volume but low value. Revenue is concentrated in longer, show-like content.

### Ultra-Long Video Revenue Decline
- 2023–2024: Ultra-long videos (120+ min) dominated — a 120-min video generated ~100x revenue of a 20-min video
- 2025: Same channels saw ultra-long video revenue share drop from **46% → 16.9%**
- New sweet spot: **45–60 minute videos** now drive ~40% of revenue

### YouTube Premium Growth
- 2020: 4% of views from Premium subscribers
- 2025: 15% of views from Premium subscribers
- Premium views deliver **1.3x RPM** vs. average views

### Platform/Device Shift
- Algorithm increasingly rewards content watched on **living-room TVs** (CTV)
- Algorithmic suggestions now drive more views than subscriptions
